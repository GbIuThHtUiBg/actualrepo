My Interest in IT

The programming part of IT really interests me, in part because what you can do with programming is limited only by the imagination. My interest in programming specifically came from playing a game.

A preface to the game (Dragalia Lost):
The game itself features characters with unique attacks, abilities and skills while pitting you against monsters that you have to defeat under a set time. As the game intends for you to repeatedly beat the bosses, a ‘META’ develops amongst players (Most Efficient Tactic Available) in order to finish the fights as quickly as possible. This would be the combinations of characters and their interactions with the bosses in the fight.

What is important to note that players are not privy to the exact values of an attack or skill and the damage formula and calculations, thus it is hard for players to determine a META in the first place. This is where datamining comes in, one of the things that I found interesting in IT. With the help of exact numbers and values (e.g., Damage calculations formula, how long in frames it takes for animations of skills to play out) a ‘DPS simulator’ was developed with the help of individuals coming together from the Dragalia Lost community. The simulator detailed how much damage per second a character dealt to bosses, which helps in determining which characters to bring to the fights, as those at the higher echelons of the graph were seen to be the best (and therefore META) characters for the fight. The simulator was coded with python, and it was eye opening seeing programming being used to establish a META, no less in a game I enjoyed playing, which is what sparked my interest in programming (and therefore IT). Prior to this I had no IT experience.

Initially, as my interest was mostly in programming, I had thought to do a Bachelor of Computer Science but decided to do study IT instead as the course at RMIT had an introduction to programming, while CS jumped right into what I thought was too advanced to go into it without any background. I chose RMIT (specifically the city campus) over Monash University as I did not want to spend four hours to and from the university on public transport; it was more convenient for me as I had a terrible experience taking the train to high school for four years that totalled two hours each day walking or on the train.

Within the first year of the course, I expect to have dipped my toes into IT, which by then I will have hopefully decided if I truly wish to continue programming or go into another part of IT. As for the rest of my studies, it still remains a mystery.

Myers-Briggs Personality Test2

From the data above, the test results suggest a personality that leans towards more being ruled by one’s emotion and thoughts. Consequent actions are as a result based on emotion rather than rationale (intuition). Using words from the survey itself, the heavy disposition towards the ‘Intuitive’ trait suggests open-mindedness, imagination and curiosity. The ‘Prospecting’ trait suggests one who is flexible, non-conforming in an effort to not limit one’s possibilities.

With regards to team behaviour, these results suggest being non-confrontational with ideas, rather, being accepting of ideas and input in the hopes that it could be developed further. It also suggests a thorough approach to completing tasks – perhaps even to perfection.

The flexibility and open-mindedness lend itself to being useful in any team. The only consideration that would need to be taken would be to not end up in a group with only open-minded introverts, as then discussion would be like trying to get jelly to bounce back a ball: It does not.

The Myers-Briggs test faces the limitation of generalising humanity’s personality down to 16 groups. In order to capture all the fish in the sea, the widest net is cast; Vague explanations and attributes were assigned that seemed ‘special’ and peculiar to an individual when in fact it is human nature and therefore common occurrence. Verbatim from the results, ‘Turbulent individuals… are likely to experience a wide range of emotions and to be success-driven’. This tactic comes off as cheap as it does not necessarily explain someone’s personality – it only tells them what it means to be human. If the point of a personality test, one named ’16 Personalities’, no less, was to distinguish people from one another by personality, then including a trait that all humans possess seems like an effort to appeal to the respondent than to provide a valid metric. This devalues the argument for the Myers-Briggs test being one that is valid, and consequently, the results that come from it.

My Project Idea

This project idea is inspired by what initially interested me regarding programming. It is to code a simulator, most likely to calculate DPS (damage per second), for a game called Brawl Stars. So far, there is only one person who seems to have done this, but they have only displayed it in a graphic with ‘x’ brawler has ‘y’ DPS. The DPS simulator for Dragalia Lost5 inspired this idea; it uses python code to crunch the numbers, then displays it with segmented bar charts with its own UI. The simulator also operates on another site where you can customise values (e.g., equipment adventurers use, enemy statistics), which is what I hope to do for this project idea but for Brawl Stars.

It will be interesting to code as the damage system for Dragalia Lost and Brawl Stars are vastly different, meaning I cannot rely entirely on the former’s open-source code for the project. It will be used as inspiration and to challenge my beginner’s programming skills.

The idea is to program, using Brawl Star’s data from the game files, a calculation for DPS of each brawler (the characters within the game).

Two different sites will be made. One focuses on the customization aspect of one brawler, for example: Some brawlers benefit from attacking from far away but are weaker up close. In this case I would account for projectile speed and distance in calculations and add it as a user input in the UI of the website. The customization aspect is implemented in the UI: there will be boxes to tick (Is this brawler getting a status that increases damage? Is it under negative effects such as reduced reload time?), a dropdown menu to pick star powers (Abilities unique to each brawler, from which they can choose only one) and other parameters. This would then update the code with whatever the user inputs and then it would run the code, calculating the customized DPS. This is especially helpful in theorycrafting for the one brawler. If possible, I would further explore the aspect of theorycrafting by adding customizations that do not use Brawl Star’s game file data. This means users can create hypothetical abilities for example, as I see very often people within the community coming up with new brawlers or adjusted ability design. This gives those people a platform to test their creativity.

The other site focuses on the comparison of brawlers displayed with a segmented bar chart. The comparison aspect will be furthered through the use of filtering in the UI. This is because there are categories which each brawler fits into which accounts for their often-unique statistics. One such category would be the ‘sharpshooters’, brawlers with long range. The filter would help in dividing the categories so as to compare the brawlers within the class itself. This matters as Brawl Stars is a 3v3 game where players fight others on ‘game modes’ each with their own ‘map’.

These game modes and maps are often different in the way in which you would play out the match and structurally respectively, resulting in certain classes being the META. If I were to make an example out of real life: a game mode would be soccer or capture the flag, while a map would be a an empty field, or a field with some walls to obstruct players, or a field with short fences placed strategically. It means that players who are better jumpers than others may favour the map with short fences. In Brawl Stars, sharpshooters often favour maps with few obstacles that are likely to provide cover for the enemies.

It helps in finding the best brawlers for a game mode where damage is king, which there are several of. Overall, it would be used as a tool to develop META’s, or even quirky hypotheticals such as ‘how can I deal exactly 14999 damage to leave the enemy’s structure at one Hit Point?

Additionally, when new brawlers are added to the game, players will be able to judge the strength of the brawler and see their place in the META. It also helps to see if brawlers are over-tuned or under-tuned (answers the question: Is this brawler balanced?), which is helpful as the game developers themselves are not as transparent with the data they share on why they adjust brawlers.

The hardware needed is anything that can run python. Python and a website are necessary for the coding and display of the data are necessary so that the public is able to use the simulator. What I have in mind is GitHub to host the website.

I need the technical coding skills and knowhow of datamining as well as HTML/CSS to accomplish making and pulling together everything for the final outcome. Much of this will be learnt, as my baseline knowledge is rather lacking, using the Dragalia Lost DPS simulator as a template for the approach to this project, as was discussed previously. The feasibility of the project idea in question is rather low, accounting for both my own lack of skill and when it comes to forming teams; the topic may be banal to some as not everyone may enjoy a game like this, it is neither Avant-Garde nor does it solve real world inconveniences.

While the outcome has been discussed in tandem with the plan and intended impact, no original problem was addressed previously. Despite this, not all solutions arise from problems with tangible effects. This project idea is more of a quality-of-life addition that gives the Brawl Stars community what they did not know they might have needed, as I have yet to see players actively asking for any of these features. Tangentially, I have seen players question the game developer’s reasons for adjusting brawlers and this may be one of many possible answers to those questions.
